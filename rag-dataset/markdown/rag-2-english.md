## Sustainable Mobility and Carbon Emission (Malaysia – Singapore Corridor)

---

# 1. PROBLEM OVERVIEW

## 1.1 Traffic Congestion Crisis

The Malaysia–Singapore region, particularly:

* Kuala Lumpur (Klang Valley)
* Johor Bahru – Singapore Causeway

is experiencing significantly worsening traffic congestion.

### Key Facts

* Congestion levels in Klang Valley increased from:

  * 37% (2019) to approximately 43.4% (2025)
* Main causes:

  * High dependence on private vehicles
  * Underdeveloped public transportation systems
  * Poor infrastructure integration

---

## 1.2 Cross-Border Bottleneck (Malaysia–Singapore)

The Johor Bahru and Singapore corridor is one of the busiest cross-border regions in Southeast Asia.

### Characteristics

* Extremely high daily commuter volume
* Severe congestion during peak hours
* Long waiting times at border checkpoints

### Contributing Factors

* Immigration processing delays
* Limited infrastructure capacity
* Imbalance in transport systems between the two countries

---

## 1.3 Transport Emissions as a Major Pollution Source

### Malaysia

* The transport sector contributes approximately 28% of total national CO2 emissions
* Around 85% of these emissions come from road transport

Implication:

* Private vehicles are the primary contributors to carbon emissions

---

### Singapore

* Transport contributes approximately 18.5% of PM2.5 air pollution
* Road transport accounts for about 43% of transport emissions
* Approximately 36% of NOx emissions come from the transport sector

---

## 1.4 Impact of the Problem

### Environmental Impact

* Increased CO2 emissions
* Air pollution (PM2.5, NOx)
* Contribution to climate change

### Economic Impact

* Reduced productivity
* High economic costs due to congestion

### Social Impact

* Commuting stress
* Declining quality of life

# 2. ROOT CAUSE ANALYSIS

## 2.1 Car Dependency

Malaysia demonstrates a high level of dependence on private vehicles.

### Key Data

* Public transport usage is approximately 25%
* Most people rely on private cars

### Impact

* Increased vehicle volume
* Higher congestion levels
* Increased emissions

---

## 2.2 Urbanization and Economic Growth

* Economic growth increases vehicle ownership
* Urban sprawl increases travel distances

### Result

* More trips are made
* Higher fuel consumption

---

## 2.3 Inefficient Public Transport

### Issues

* Uneven coverage
* Low reliability
* Poor integration between transport modes

### Impact

* Users prefer private vehicles

---

## 2.4 Fossil Fuel Dependency

### Singapore

* Approximately 89% of transport energy is still derived from fossil fuels

### Impact

* Emissions remain high despite efficiency improvements

---

# 3. TRANSPORTATION SYSTEM ANALYSIS

## 3.1 Singapore (Highly Optimized System)

### Strengths

* Near-complete public transport accessibility within a 500-meter radius
* Rapidly expanding MRT system
* Strong transport policies:

  * Vehicle quota system
  * Congestion pricing

### Impact

* Controlled vehicle ownership
* Lower emissions

---

## 3.2 Malaysia (Developing System)

### Weaknesses

* Public transport is not fully optimized
* Uneven infrastructure development
* Fragmented governance

### Impact

* High car dependency
* High congestion levels
